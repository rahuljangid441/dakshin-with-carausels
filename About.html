Global South Centre of Excellence is the culmination of India’s vision to have a formalized institution. The idea for this mechanism of mobilizing nations from the Global South was articulated in January 2023 by Hon'ble Prime Minister of India Shri Narendra Modi.

 

Established as a part of Research and Information System for Developing Countries (RIS), GSCE endeavors to assist relevant stakeholders and partners in the identification and implementation of scalable and sustainable solutions that can transform the economies and societies of Global South countries.

Global South Centre of Excellence
Foster knowledge sharing and mutual learning for building capacities and developing sustainable, affordable, and locally relevant solutions by collating resources, experiences and strategies to overcome developmental challenges the Global South faces, and promote globally inclusive partnerships.
The Centre, in association with its institutional partners, would facilitate dialogue, handholding and support to identified and mutually agreed projects in the Global South under the overall guidance of the Governments and agencies concerned. The Centre would carry out research, foster sharing of mutually enriching development experiences and success stories from the Global South, document these research and experiences, and connect with governments, businesses, academia, think tanks, and other stakeholders to promote Lifestyle For Environment (LiFE) and other sustainable development initiatives under the overarching ambit of Agenda 2030 and beyond. The Centre would evolve a database of development cooperation for longer-term engagements and policy advice.

Voice of Global South Summit 2023

The Voice of Global South Summit (VoGS), first-of-its-kind forum started by India during its G20 Presidency, holds profound significance. This summit provides a platform for emerging economies and nations from the Global South to express their unique perspectives, challenges, and innovative solutions on the global stage. Initiated with the goal to amplify the voices of low- and middle-income countries, the summit leverages its influence to call upon developed economies to re-orient their approach toward the underserved. It fosters a more inclusive and equitable dialogue and acts as a catalyst for collaboration, offering fresh insights and driving collective action towards a more balanced and interconnected world.

At the VoGS, Prime Minister of India announced five key initiatives. These initiatives are true reflection of India’s development philosophy anchored in knowledge sharing and capacity building. The contours of these initiatives are presented below:
Key Initiatives
Global South Young Diplomats Forum

In an increasingly complex world, a major burden falls on diplomats who navigate dynamic yet challenging situations. Young diplomats from Global South earnestly need a platform for frank engagement, and exchange of views and experience as well as mentorship for deeper understanding of issues and future preparedness. Newly launched efforts by India in the spirit of demand driven development cooperation with partner countries.

Global South Science & Technology Initiative

India has emerged as a source of technological and digital solutions ensuring access, equity and inclusion. India’s space missions, and capabilities and innovations in digital public infrastructure, fintech, renewable energy among others would form potential bridges of lasting and mutually rewarding partnerships through sharing of expertise in the Global South.

Aarogya Maitri

India is considered pharmacy of the world and emerged as a true friend of the Global South through ‘Vaccine Maitri’ at the peak of the pandemic. On numerous occasions India has rushed medical supplies and personnel at times of natural disasters and on humanitarian grounds. India shall maintain high standard of operational preparedness to deliver such support in the future as well.

Global South Scholarships

India is considered a global hub of higher education and research across disciplines attracting students from many countries of the Global South. India shall scale up financial resources to support increased number of scholarships in an effort to create human resource capabilities in the Global South.
