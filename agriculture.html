In the spirit of ‘One Earth, One Family, One Future’ Global South Cooperation in agriculture can fast-track to attain food security and nutrition, sustainable agriculture with climate smart approach, inclusive agri-value chains and food systems, all of which necessitate digitalization for agricultural transformation.

Sustainable, Inclusive and Climate-Resilient Agriculture for resource efficiency, risk management and livelihoods protection
Flagship Programmes

This is a programme for achieving high growth in agricultural sector, higher returns to the farmers and for integrated development by focusing on food security, sustainable agriculture, production of oil seeds and agricultural extension. It includes promotion of nutrition-rich crops including millets, pulses, etc. This programme can help other countries of Global South to evolve programmes towards achieving food security and nutrition. In India, the programme is domestically known as Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana.

For more information click here

National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA) has been formulated for enhancing agricultural productivity, especially in rainfed areas focusing on integrated farming, water use efficiency, soil health management and synergizing resource conservation. Learnings from this programme can help in achieving sustainability in rain-fed areas for other countries of Global South through optimum use of available inputs.

For more information click here

The scheme aims to provide insurance coverage and financial support to the farmers in the event of failure of any of the notified crops as a result of natural calamities, pests & diseases. It also aims at stabilising incomes, adopting innovative and modern agricultural practices and to ensure flow of credit. Such a risk management mechanism can prove useful for other countries of Global South in stabilizing incomes of farmers, especially in view of the kind of risks involved in agriculture. In India, the programme is domestically known as Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana.

For more information click here

Soil Health Cards provide information to farmers on nutrient status of their soil along with recommendation on appropriate dosage of nutrients to be applied for improving soil health and its fertility. Adoption of such soil health cards can facilitate better soil health management, while averting excessive use of fertilizers and promoting sustainability in other countries of the Global South.

For more information click here

This programme provides medium to long term debt financing facility for investment in viable projects for post-harvest management infrastructure and community farming assets. Other countries of Global South may find the modalities of this investment programme useful in channelizing credit for creation of agricultural infrastructure.

For more information click here

The scheme is meant to contribute to enhance cost effective productivity and higher net incomes to the member farmer producers groups through better liquidity and market linkages for their produce and to help FPOs to become sustainable through collective action. This scheme can help in harnessing economies of scale across the agri-value chain in other countries of Global South.

For more information click here

National Agriculture Market (eNAM) is a pan-India electronic trading portal which networks the existing Agriculture Produce Market Committee (APMC) mandis to create a unified national market for agricultural commodities. This intervention can help other countries of Global South in creating seamless platforms to reduce asymmetric information in agricultural marketing.

For more information click here

Agri Stack is the digital foundation meant to make it easier to bring various stakeholders together to improve agriculture in India and enable better outcomes and results for the farmers by using data and digital services. The infrastructure and foundational layer of Agri Stack can enable other countries of Global South to provide farmers with tailored services through access to high-quality, validated and attested data.

For more information click here

It facilitates to unleash the power of mobile in the hands of farmers to get information/advisories as per his or her preferences in the form of text/ voice messages and getting access to numerous databases even without internet. Such a portal can act as a game changer by harnessing the extensive availability of mobile telephony, to keep the farmers of other countries of Global South updated.
