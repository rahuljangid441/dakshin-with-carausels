The COVID-19 pandemic engineered fundamental shifts in public health and forced nations to reimagine the healthcare landscape. As emerging economies find innovative ways of addressing their challenges, and bolstering their systems, they are uniquely poised to work together and thus progress together. India – with its successful implementation of the world’s largest vaccination drive and deployment of initiatives like Vaccine Maitri to help other countries in need – now has a repository of good practices that can be invaluable to any country seeking to strengthen its health systems.

Health Systems Strengthening is crucial for improving global health outcomes, focusing on accessibility, affordability, and quality healthcare availability.

Maternal and Child Health is a crucial aspect to achieve SDGs and Universal Health Coverage. Key components include comprehensive immunization, improved nutrition, and adolescent health initiatives, etc. disease prevention, and a resilient future.

 

Non-Communicable Diseases affect people of all ages, regions, and countries. Countries are adopting a comprehensive approach to early detect, screen, and treat these diseases, by providing palliative care, treatment for overall well-being & sustainable healthcare systems.

 

Digital Health Solutions are crucial for efficient care delivery, enhancing patient care, remote monitoring, and optimizing supply chains.

Communicable Diseases , including HIV/AIDS, TB, malaria, and neglected diseases, significantly impact resource-constrained communities and impose economic and social costs.

 

Flagship Programmes

National Health Protection Scheme is the largest health assurance scheme in the world which aims at providing a health cover of Rs. 5 lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization to over 12 crores poor and vulnerable families (approximately 55 crore beneficiaries) that form the bottom 40% of the Indian population. In India, the programme is domestically known as Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY.

For more information click here

Health Infrastructure Digital Mission aims to develop the backbone necessary to support the integrated digital health infrastructure of the country. It will bridge the existing gap amongst different stakeholders of Healthcare ecosystem through digital highways. In India, the programme is domestically known as Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM).

For more information click here

Co-WIN Global Outreach Program - The Co-WIN platform has been made available as a “Digital Public Good” for all the nations globally. It is scalable, inclusive and open platform that serves the entire public health system of nation.

For more information click here

Bridging the Digital Health Divide eSanjeevani - National Telemedicine Service of India is a step towards digital health equity to achieve Universal Health Coverage (UHC). In India, the programme is domestically known as E-Sanjeevani.

For more information click here

The National Health Mission (NHM) encompasses its two Sub-Missions, the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) and the newly launched National Urban Health Mission (NUHM). The main programmatic components include Health System Strengthening in rural and urban areas- Reproductive-Maternal- Neonatal-Child and Adolescent Health (RMNCH+A), and Communicable and Non-Communicable Diseases.
