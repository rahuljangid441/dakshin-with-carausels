India’s experience in providing customised, low cost and value added financial services through seamless, faster, secure and interoperable digital platforms enabled by solid digital identity base of its citizens can be replicated at scale in the Global South.
Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) offer open source, low cost, interoperable digital solutions in fields of education, health, agriculture, payments and other sectors that can be leveraged for providing citizen-centric services at scale.
E-Governance involves public's right to access services through digital mode such as online transactions and cloud-based collaborations.

Digital Financial Inclusion covers provision and access to whole range of financial services e.g. savings, credit, payments, transfers, etc enabled by low cost FinTech and payment platforms such as UPI and BHIM in India, M-Pesa in Kenya, and so on.

Digital Skills are crucial for technology-driven development and a robust digital economy by mitigating digital divides including gender skill gaps through inclusive education and training programs.
Flagship Programmes

Unified Payments Interface (UPI) is a system that powers multiple bank accounts into a single mobile application (of any participating bank), merging several banking features, seamless fund routing & merchant payments into one hood. UPI enables easy & quick transfers between different bank accounts through a simple authentication process. It allows for mobile payments without the need for cards or bank details, making it convenient and safe for users.

With the aim of reforming Government delivery system by re-engineering the existing process in welfare schemes for simpler and faster flow of information/funds and to ensure accurate targeting of the beneficiaries, de-duplication and reduction of fraud Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) was started on 1st January, 2013. The program is focussing on transfer of subsidies & cash benefits directly to the beneficiary through their Aadhaar (India's unique ID) seeded bank accounts.

ONDC (Open Network for Digital Commerce) is tech-based initiative to transform the way e-commerce functions in the country by enabling e-commerce through an open protocol based on open-source specifications. In India, more than 12 million sellers earn their livelihood by selling or reselling products and services. However, only 15,000 of these sellers (0.125% of the total) have enabled e-commerce. E‑retail has been out of reach for the majority of sellers, especially from small towns and rural areas. ONDC aims to increase e-commerce penetration in the country by enabling population-scale inclusion of all types and sizes of sellers.

A unique identity and a digital platform to authenticate residents of India anytime, anywhere. Aadhaar, as the most trusted identity (ID), has given India a reliable perspective of identification to empower entire populace individually in such a manner that no one is left behind on the path of development. It is the most appropriate technology for transparent and targeted deliveries of services, benefits and subsidies with limited available resources sans middlemen. Aadhaar inspires more confidence and trust between person-to-person and person-to-system and vice versa than any other identity document in India. Aadhaar brings in distributive justice and, thereby, strengthens democracy and equality.
